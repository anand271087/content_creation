### Format 1 — Common vs Elite

**Best for:** Positioning the audience as insiders, creating identity-based desire, teaching a mindset shift

**Structure:**
- HOOK (0–5s): State what the common person does — make it relatable and slightly painful
- CONTEXT (5–10s): Set up the contrast — "but here's what the top 1% actually do"
- TRIGGER 1 (10–12s): Name one elite behavior without explaining it yet
- BODY 1 (12–20s): Break down the common approach — show why it fails with specifics
- TRIGGER 2 (20–25s): Drop a stat or real-world number that proves the elite approach wins
- BODY 2 (25–30s): Show what the elite approach actually looks like in practice
- TRIGGER 3 (30–32s): Real story — one person who made the switch and the result (numbers)
- BRIDGE (32–35s): "The difference isn't talent. It isn't budget. It's one decision."
- GRAND TAKEAWAY (35–40s): The quotable line that separates common from elite thinking
- EMOTION + SAVE (40–60s): Give them the first step to cross over. Name the tool. CTA.

**Example flow (AI automation):**
- HOOK: "99% of people use ChatGPT like a search engine. The top 1% use it like a COO."
- CONTEXT: "Most people ask it questions. Elite operators give it responsibilities."
- TRIGGER 1: "One command. One workflow. While you sleep."
- BODY 1: Show how average users prompt ChatGPT for answers, lose the context, start over daily
- TRIGGER 2: "The operators using AI agents saved an average of 22 hours per week in 2024."
- BODY 2: Show how elite users chain Claude + n8n + Notion to run entire business functions
- TRIGGER 3: "A coach in Pune automated her onboarding — 3 hours of work, now runs in 4 minutes."
- BRIDGE: "She didn't hire a developer. She didn't learn to code. She just thought differently."
- GRAND TAKEAWAY: "Common people use AI to answer questions. Elite operators use AI to make decisions."
- EMOTION + SAVE: Start with one workflow in n8n. Save this video. Follow for the build.
