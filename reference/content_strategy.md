## PART 1 — GROWTH STRATEGY (how to actually get followers)

You've proven the engine works (keyword-CTA reel: 44 comments vs 2 likes on a soft-CTA; board formats out-reach talking heads 5–10×). At 43 followers the problem is **volume + distribution + connection**, not more format experiments.

### The 3 levers, in priority order
1. **Distribution (biggest gap, zero extra work).** Every reel → IG Reels + YouTube Shorts + LinkedIn + Facebook + a Threads post. IG itself says cross-posting is the #1 untapped reach lever. You already generate all four captions. **Do it on the next post.**
2. **Consistency at volume.** Breakouts come *after* months of shipping; the winner reveals itself. Target **4–7 reels/week**, rotating formats so the feed isn't one recording session.
3. **Connection layered on utility.** Utility (dense boards) earns the *save*; talking-to-camera + a lived story earns the *follow*. Add one personal line per reel ("I built this because I was drowning in X") + reply to every comment in the first 60 min.

### Platform-specific (different games)
- **Instagram** — home base. Board reels + keyword CTA + first-hour replies. Optimize saves & comments.
- **YouTube Shorts** — same file, **searchable titles** ("n8n tutorial for beginners 2026"). Long evergreen tail; `effect_recipe` tutorials over-perform here.
- **LinkedIn** — different tone: no keyword-CTA, no hype. **PDF carousels = 6.6% engagement (highest format); personal profile beats company page 561%.** March 2026 "Authenticity Update" killed engagement-bait/pods. Post opinionated POV, ROI case studies, "death of X" essays from Anand's personal profile.

### Positioning (the unfair advantage)
Be **"the procurement operator who automated his own job and shows founders how"** — not another "AI tips" account. Your corporate-procurement + AI-builder intersection is a lane nobody else in the niche owns, and it's pure gold on LinkedIn (B2B trusts an operator, not a guru).

### The India edge (live in the data)
Your 22–45 India professional audience is on the **supply side** of the automation gold rush (building/selling automations) AND under live **layoff fear** (India = #2 country for AI layoffs, ~28–30K jobs cut May 2026 alone). Two proven emotional drivers to alternate: **fear** ("reskill or replaced") and **opportunity** ("charge more, don't get commoditized"). Indian freelancers sell n8n gigs at $150–250 — "how not to race to the bottom" lands harder than "AI is cool."

## PART 2 — TOPIC-GENERATION ENGINE (repeatable, use every week)

**Step 1 — Pick a live signal** from one of 4 buckets (refresh monthly via the same 2-agent sweep):
- **Debate** (comment-bait) → verdict_board / sort_board / swap_board / contrast_stack
- **Hyped vs dying tool** → verdict_board "future or finished" / tier_board
- **"How do I…" question** → effect_recipe tutorial / countdown / checklist
- **Money/fear angle** → authority hook + keyword-CTA + lead magnet

**Step 2 — Map to a format** using the bucket above (decision engine in `formats/registry.py`).

**Step 3 — Write the hook** = emotional trigger + specific number/tool + curiosity gap, in 3 seconds. Rotate 5–7 patterns (reusing one drops effectiveness ~45%):
- "I let AI [do my job] for [N weeks] — here's what happened"
- "OpenAI/Google just quietly launched ___"
- "You're still [old behavior]. The winners already [new behavior]."
- "[N] jobs gone in [timeframe]. The survivors did ONE thing."
- "This [₹2,000/free] tool replaced my [employee]."
- "[Tool A] or [Tool B]? Pick wrong and you waste 3 months."
- "Steal my [system]" + "Save this."

**Step 4 — Attach a keyword CTA + lead magnet** if it's a save/lead play (not on LinkedIn).

**Step 5 — QA gates before ship:** sound-off test · framework visible frame 1 (boards) · ends on payoff · one personal line · cross-post captions ready.
