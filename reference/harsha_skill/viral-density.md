# Viral Density — the gold standard

Viral scripts must be **very dense**: maximum intrigue per second, zero dead air,
**max 15 seconds** (~35-45 words). Model every viral script on the example below.

## Gold-standard example (Harsha)

> "This is going to be the craziest video you watch this year. Because you probably
> had no idea that what you're watching is the AI version of me talking. I recorded
> myself one time, and now this version of me shows up online every single day, while
> I'm off running my business or spending time with my family. Follow me and I'll
> teach you exactly how I built this."

Why it works — four beats, one sentence each, each doing a **different** job:
1. **Bold-claim hook** — "the craziest video you watch this year." Instant scroll-stop.
2. **The reveal** — "what you're watching is the AI version of me." Intrigue delivered
   in the *second* line, not saved for later.
3. **Proof + dream fused** — "recorded once… shows up every day, while I'm running my
   business or spending time with my family." Mechanism (film once) AND the emotional
   payoff (freedom, family) in a single sentence.
4. **CTA with a promise** — "Follow me and I'll teach you exactly how I built this."
   Not just "follow" — follow to close a curiosity loop.

Note what he **left out**: no "3 reasons it looks fake" teaching. That's Authority
content. The viral cut stays pure intrigue.

## The viral beat structure (copy this)
`Hook (bold/reveal)` → `Reveal or Payoff` → `Proof + Dream (one line)` → `CTA + promise`

## Before / after (density in action)

**Loose (too wordy, ~25s):**
> "You just watched an AI talk for ten seconds and couldn't tell. This isn't a filter,
> and it isn't a deepfake. I recorded myself one time, and now this version of me shows
> up online every day while I'm off running my business. The reason you couldn't tell
> it's AI isn't the tool — it's three things: real footage, a proper voice clone, and a
> script written the way I talk. So if you hate being on camera, you don't have to
> choose. Follow if you'd rather build the business than film all day."

**Dense (~14s):**
> "You just watched an AI for ten seconds and couldn't tell. On purpose. I filmed
> myself once — now I post every day while I'm with my family. Think it looks fake? It
> won't if it's built right. Follow and I'll show you exactly how."

What changed: cut "isn't a filter / deepfake" (setup), removed the whole 3-reasons
teach (that's Authority), fused proof + dream ("filmed once… with my family"),
CTA now carries a promise. ~40% shorter, every line lands.

## Checklist for any viral script
- [ ] ≤ 15 seconds (~35-45 words)
- [ ] One sentence per beat; 4 beats
- [ ] Bold claim + reveal in the first two lines
- [ ] Proof and dream fused into one line
- [ ] CTA carries a promise ("…and I'll show you how")
- [ ] No teaching / how-to breakdown (that belongs in Authority)
- [ ] Every line does a different job; nothing repeats or just transitions
