# Hook Formulas

The hook is the first 3 seconds. It is three things at once: the **scripted** hook
(what you say), the **visual** hook (text overlay in the first 0.5s, high contrast,
24pt+), and the **auditory** hook (whoosh / pop / record-scratch). Negative and
contrarian hooks outperform positive ones by 40-60% — prefer them.

Every hook must pass 5 tests: Scroll-Stop ("would this stop ME?"), Clarity (clear what
it's about), Curiosity (opens a knowledge gap), Relevance (hits the ICP's pain),
Uniqueness (not a worn-out angle).

## The 5 core formulas

### 1. Negative Hook  (strongest performer)
Pattern: "Stop [doing X] if you [want the thing they want]."
- "Stop posting your face online if you actually value your time."
- "Stop making content if you don't have a way to capture leads."

### 2. Curiosity Gap
Pattern: "[Surprising statement] — and here's why…"
- "99% of founders advertise wrong, and here's why."
- "Most AI avatars look fake for one reason, and it's not the video."

### 3. Direct Challenge
Pattern: "You're probably doing [common thing] wrong."
- "You're probably writing your hooks wrong."
- "Your content strategy is backwards."

### 4. Contrarian
Pattern: "Everyone says [common belief] — they're wrong."
- "Everyone says post daily. They're wrong."
- "They tell you to just be on camera more. That's the worst advice."

### 5. The "But Wait" Flip
Pattern: state the expected, then flip it.
- "Everyone knows you should post daily… but what if that's actually hurting you?"

## Advanced hook types (use sparingly)

- **Personal story:** "This one mistake cost me a client and 6 months."
- **Data-driven:** "I studied 1,000 founder reels and found one pattern."
- **Transformation:** "6 months ago I never posted. Now I post daily and never film."

## For the ZeroHands ICP

Lead the hook with the founder's real pain or doubt:
- being invisible / losing customers to visible competitors
- hating being on camera
- thinking AI avatars look fake
- posting but getting no clients (the funnel gap)
